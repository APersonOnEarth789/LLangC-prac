Blockchain

A blockchain is a distributed digital ledger that records transactions across many computers. 
Instead of a central authority maintaining records, each participant in the network holds a copy of the ledger.

Transactions are grouped into blocks. Each block is cryptographically linked to the previous block, forming a chain.

This design makes blockchains resistant to tampering, because altering a block would require modifying every subsequent block across the entire network.

Key properties of blockchains include:
- decentralization
- transparency
- immutability
- cryptographic security

Bitcoin was the first successful blockchain implementation.
