Ethereum is a decentralized blockchain platform that enables developers to build and deploy smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps).

Unlike Bitcoin, which primarily focuses on digital currency, Ethereum was designed as a programmable blockchain. Developers can write programs that automatically execute when predefined conditions are met.

Smart contracts are self-executing pieces of code stored on the Ethereum blockchain. They enable trustless transactions between parties without requiring intermediaries.

Ethereum introduced the concept of a virtual machine called the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM). The EVM allows smart contracts to run on the blockchain in a secure and deterministic way.

Ether (ETH) is the native cryptocurrency of the Ethereum network. It is used to pay transaction fees known as gas.

Gas fees compensate network validators for processing and validating transactions. Complex smart contract operations require more gas.

Ethereum transitioned from Proof of Work to Proof of Stake in an upgrade known as "The Merge". In Proof of Stake, validators stake ETH to help secure the network.

Ethereum is the foundation for many decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols, NFT marketplaces, and blockchain-based applications.
